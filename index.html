Overview

About SecDLT

Distributed ledger technologies (DLTs) are increasingly being incorporated into IoT, 5G/6G, vehicular networks, and edge computing systems, enabling decentralized trust, secure data management, and automated governance. However, this integration simultaneously creates new and underexplored security and privacy risks at the intersection of DLT and communication network infrastructure.

SecDLT is a full-day workshop that serves as a dedicated platform to address these challenges. It brings together researchers and practitioners in distributed computing, network security, and applied cryptography to identify open problems, present new research, and create opportunities for future collaborations.

Publication

Accepted papers will appear in the ACM International Conference Proceeding Series (ICPS), indexed by ACM, DBLP, and Scopus, as a companion volume to the main conference proceedings.

Best Paper Award

A Best Paper Award will be presented at the closing session, selected by the program committee based on technical quality, originality, and relevance.

Scope

Topics of Interest

We invite original, unpublished research contributions on topics related to DLT-enabled networks, including, but not limited to:

Smart Contract and Consensus Security
  • Smart contract security, formal verification, and runtime monitoring
  • Consensus mechanism security in resource-constrained and uncertain environments
  • Blockchain-based intrusion and anomaly detection
Identity, Interoperability, and Network Security
  • Decentralized identity (DID), authentication, and access control
  • Secure cross-domain DLT interoperability in heterogeneous deployments
  • Security and privacy in vehicular, smart grid, and smart city networks
  • Scalability, performance, and security trade-offs in DLT-based systems
Privacy, Cryptography, and Compliance
  • Privacy-preserving protocols: ZKPs, SNARKs, STARKs, zkVMs, and MPC
  • Post-quantum cryptography for distributed ledger communications
  • Privacy-preserving data sharing, auditing, and provenance on distributed ledgers
  • Regulatory compliance and data sovereignty in DLT-enabled infrastructures

Authors

Submission Guidelines

Paper Format

All submissions must be written in English and formatted according to the ACM double-column conference style. Papers must be submitted as PDF files.

Full Papers: 6–10 pages (including references)

Short Papers: 4–6 pages (including references)

Review Process

Double-blind review — author names, affiliations, and self-identifying references must be removed. All papers receive a minimum of 3 reviews.

Acceptance rate capped at 40% per ICDCN steering committee policy.

Registration

ICDCN 2027 follows a single registration fee for the entire 4-day event. There is no workshop-only registration. At least one author must register and present in person.

Publication

Papers published via ACM ICPS. Authors not in the ACM Open program may be required to pay an Article Processing Charge (APC).
