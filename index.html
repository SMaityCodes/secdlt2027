Overview

About SecDLT

Distributed ledger technologies (DLTs) are increasingly being incorporated into IoT, 5G/6G, vehicular networks, and edge computing systems, enabling decentralized trust, secure data management, and automated governance. However, this integration simultaneously creates new and underexplored security and privacy risks at the intersection of DLT and communication network infrastructure.

SecDLT is a full-day workshop that provides a dedicated forum to address these challenges rigorously. It brings together researchers and practitioners from distributed computing, network security, and applied cryptography to surface open problems, present original research, and catalyze new collaborations. The workshop features a keynote talk, three refereed paper sessions, and a closing open discussion on emerging research directions.

Workshop papers will be published in the ACM International Conference Proceeding Series (ICPS), indexed by ACM, DBLP, and Scopus.

Scope

Topics of Interest

We invite original, unpublished research contributions on topics including, but not limited to:

  • Smart contract security, formal verification, and runtime monitoring
  • Consensus mechanism security in resource-constrained environments
  • Privacy-preserving protocols: ZKPs, STARKs, zkVMs, and MPC
  • Secure cross-domain DLT interoperability in heterogeneous deployments
  • Decentralized identity (DID), authentication, and access control for IoT and zero-trust architectures
  • Privacy-preserving data sharing, auditing, and provenance on distributed ledgers
  • Post-quantum cryptography for distributed ledger communications
  • Blockchain-based intrusion and anomaly detection
  • Regulatory compliance and data sovereignty in DLT-enabled infrastructures
  • Security and privacy in vehicular, smart grid, and smart city networks
  • Scalability, performance, and security trade-offs in DLT-based systems

Authors

Submission Guidelines

Template

All submissions must use the official LaTeX ACM article template (acmart.cls, v2.16+):

\documentclass[sigconf,authordraft]{acmart}

Templates available at acm.org/publications/proceedings-template.

Paper Length

Regular Papers: Up to 10 pages (double-column, including figures, tables, and references). A clearly marked appendix for supplementary material is permitted.

Short Papers: Up to 5 pages (double-column, including all content).

Review Process

Double-blind review — author names, affiliations, and email addresses must not appear anywhere in the submission. Use third-person references to your prior work. Minimum 3 reviews per submission. Acceptance rate capped at 40% per ICDCN steering committee policy.

Generative AI Policy

Use of generative AI tools is permitted in accordance with ACM guidelines.

Publication

Accepted papers will appear in the ACM ICPS companion proceedings. At least one author must register and present in person. Authors at institutions not in the ACM Open program may be subject to an Article Processing Charge (APC). See ACM author guidance.
